1. Question #17 [fill] [Medium]
Q: The two key documents that contain the rights and freedoms of Canadians are the Charter of Rights and Freedoms and the ________ Act.
✓ Answer: Canadian Human Rights
Explanation: The Canadian Human Rights Act complements the Charter by prohibiting discrimination in federal jurisdiction.


2. Question #14 [fill] [Medium]
Q: The right to move freely within Canada is known as ________ rights.
✓ Answer: mobility
Explanation: Mobility rights guarantee citizens and permanent residents the right to move and take up residence anywhere in Canada.


3. Question #921 [boolean] [Easy]
Q: Canada has freedom of the press protected by the Charter.
✓ Answer: True
Explanation: Freedom of the press is protected under Section 2(b) of the Charter — freedom of thought, belief, opinion, and expression, including freedom of the press.


4. Question #368 [fill] [Easy]
Q: The ________ duty means new citizens should learn about Canada's history, values, and institutions.
✓ Answer: civic
Explanation: A key civic duty of citizenship is to learn about and understand Canada's history, values, and how it is governed.


5. Question #491 [boolean] [Medium]
Q: The Canadian government can limit Charter rights if it can justify doing so in a free and democratic society.
✓ Answer: True
Explanation: Section 1 (the limitations clause) allows governments to place 'reasonable limits' on Charter rights if they can be 'demonstrably justified in a free and democratic society.'

Justice System (69 total questions)
================================================================================

1. Question #914 [fill] [Hard]
Q: A ________ offense in Canada is one that can be prosecuted as either summary or indictable at the Crown's discretion.
✓ Answer: hybrid (dual)
Explanation: Hybrid offences are crimes that can be treated as either summary (minor) or indictable (serious) — the Crown chooses based on the circumstances.


2. Question #539 [boolean] [Medium]
Q: A person can be tried twice for the same crime in Canada.
✓ Answer: False
Explanation: The rule of 'double jeopardy' (protection in Section 11(h) of the Charter) prevents a person from being tried twice for the same offence once acquitted or convicted.


3. Question #541 [fill] [Hard]
Q: The ________ Court is the trial court of general jurisdiction in most Canadian provinces.
✓ Answer: Superior (Provincial Superior Court)
Explanation: Superior Courts are the main trial courts in each province, handling serious criminal and civil cases.


4. Question #543 [single] [Medium]
Q: What is parole in the Canadian justice system?
   A. The reduction of a prison sentence for good behaviour during trial
   B. Conditional early release from prison under supervision
   C. An alternative to prison for first-time offenders
   D. A form of community service
✓ Answer: B
Explanation: Parole is the supervised conditional release of a prisoner before their full sentence is served, monitored by the Parole Board of Canada.


5. Question #544 [fill] [Medium]
Q: The ________ Board of Canada decides whether federal offenders can be released before the end of their sentence.
✓ Answer: Parole
Explanation: The Parole Board of Canada grants, denies, and revokes parole and other forms of conditional release for federal offenders.
